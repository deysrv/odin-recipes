Jalebi

Description

Jalebi is a popular Indian sweet made by deep-frying a batter in spiral shapes and then soaking them in warm sugar syrup. Itâ€™s crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Typically served warm, Jalebi is enjoyed during festivals or as a snack with curd.

Ingredients:

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Rice flour (for crispiness)
  • Turmeric or yellow food color
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cardamom powder (optional)
  • Ghee or oil (for frying)

Steps:

  1. Prepare a batter by mixing flour, rice flour, and water. Add a pinch of turmeric or food color for the traditional yellow color.
  2. Let the batter rest for about 1-2 hours to ferment slightly.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and use a squeeze bottle or piping bag to form spiral shapes of the batter into the hot oil.
  4. Fry until golden and crispy, then immediately soak the jalebis in warm sugar syrup flavored with cardamom.
  5. Serve hot, optionally with a side of curd or milk.
